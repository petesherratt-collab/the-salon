His most famous dictum , " War is the continuation of politics by other means ," emphasizes that military operations must always serve a rational political objective . In this framework , the political object the original motive for the war determines both the military objective and the amount of effort required to reach it . Clausewitz utilized a dialectical method to explore the tension between " absolute or ideal war " and " real war ". Absolute war is the theoretical , unbound manifestation of violence , where two forces collide with a tendency toward unlimited destruction . However , in reality , war is always moderated by human factors , environmental conditions , and the interplay of chance . This " real war " is characterized by its probabilistic nature and the fact that its results are never final . To explain the complex nature of war , Clausewitz introduced the concept of the " paradoxical trinity " ( wunderliche Dreifaltigkeit ). This trinity consists of three fundamental tendencies that are present in every war , though their proportions vary . 1. Primordial Violence , Hatred , and Enmity : This is a blind natural force , primarily associated with the passions of the people . 2. The Play of Chance and Probability : This is the realm where the creative spirit of the commander and the army is free to operate within the uncertainty of conflict . 3. Subordination to Policy : This is the element of reason , representing the government ' s role in directing the war toward a political end . Modern interpretations , such as those by Harry Summers , often simplify this as " people , army , and government ," but Clausewitz s original intent was more abstract , focusing on the psychological and functional forces of passion , chance , and reason . Identifying this culminating point is a vital task for any commander to prevent overextension and subsequent defeat .
