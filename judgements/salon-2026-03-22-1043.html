This question concerns the ordering of society when economic utility no longer distributes honour or purpose — a crisis not of wealth but of dignity and civic place.

Men require more than bread; they require distinction, routine, and the sense of being necessary to something beyond themselves. When work provided these, it also disciplined the masses and gave them stake in social order. The state must manufacture new sources of status and structure, or watch disorder manufacture itself. This means inventing civic roles, public distinctions, and daily disciplines that mimic work's stabilising effects without its economic pretext.
